# Problem Statement

## Background

Rapid urbanization and climate change have significantly intensified the **Urban Heat Island (UHI)** effect, where urban areas experience substantially higher temperatures than surrounding rural regions.

The replacement of natural landscapes with concrete structures, asphalt roads, and dense infrastructure reduces natural cooling while increasing heat absorption and retention. As a result, cities face rising temperatures that adversely impact public health, energy consumption, environmental sustainability, and overall quality of life.

## The Challenge

Urban planners and policymakers often struggle to answer critical questions such as:

- Which areas are most vulnerable to extreme heat?
- How will heat patterns evolve over time?
- Which mitigation strategies provide the greatest impact?
- How can limited resources be allocated efficiently?

Current approaches typically rely on historical observations and static analysis, making it difficult to proactively respond to changing climate conditions.

Furthermore, urban heat is influenced by multiple interconnected factors, including:

- Land Surface Temperature (LST)
- Vegetation Cover (NDVI)
- Surface Albedo
- Land Use / Land Cover (LULC)
- Urban Morphology
- Population Density
- Weather Conditions

Analyzing these diverse datasets manually is both time-consuming and computationally challenging.

## Objective

Develop an AI-powered decision support platform capable of:

- Detecting urban heat hotspots using satellite imagery and geospatial data.
- Forecasting future heat stress using machine learning.
- Recommending optimal cooling interventions through multi-objective optimization.
- Simulating "what-if" urban planning scenarios using a digital twin.
- Supporting governments and urban planners in making informed, data-driven decisions.

## Expected Outcome

The proposed solution aims to help cities:

- Reduce urban heat risks
- Improve public health and safety
- Enhance climate resilience
- Optimize resource allocation
- Promote sustainable urban development

By combining geospatial intelligence, Artificial Intelligence, and optimization techniques, the system transforms urban heat mitigation from a reactive process into a proactive and predictive planning framework.
